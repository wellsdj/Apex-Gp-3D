HOW TO PLAY

  • Click & hold one of your players, pull back for power, release to flick it at the ball.
  • Possession follows the last touch: whoever touched the ball last flicks next — a deflection off an opponent hands them the ball. Gentle touches let you dribble upfield.
  • Limited touches in a row by the same player (2–4, set on the home screen) — then you must pass. One touch too many is a foul: free kick where it happened.
  • Smashing into an opponent before touching the ball is a foul → free kick (in your own box: PENALTY). A soft nudge is play on.
  • Throw-ins & corners: you get 20 seconds to reposition any of your players first (drag them around, then press TAKE IT NOW). The pause is added to the half as stoppage time. The taker can't touch the ball twice in a row — someone else must touch it first.
  • Ball over the sideline: throw-in. Over the goal line: corner or goal kick, by last touch.
  • Restart power varies: throw-ins are weak, corners stronger, goal kicks go long — and each player throws a little differently.
  • Your keeper is special: grab it with the mouse at any time and slide it along the goal — but it can only move so fast, and it never leaves its line. The computer's keeper plays by the same limits: it reacts late and dives imperfectly.
  • Set difficulty, half length, touch limit, team names and kit colours on the home screen.
  • Kick-offs must be passed backwards — at the start, after goals, and at half time.
  • Two halves with a running clock. Match stats at half time and full time.
